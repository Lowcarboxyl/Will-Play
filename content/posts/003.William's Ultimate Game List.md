Fire Emblem Radiant Dawn (2007)

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (1).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Fire Emblem Radiant Dawn for the Wii</div>
</div>

It almost killed IS, and it destroyed my media world too. Unfortunately, whenever I see any multi-sided conflict in a game, I can’t help but think of this game and how it created a multi-perspective conflict where the individual strengths of each side are apparent through the mechanics in-game.
That feeling of seeing a bunch of buff mercs on the other side of the map was SCARY to me. It even made the legendary hoarding 12-year-old me capitulate to using my weapons to kill basic enemies.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (2).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">yes its a relic of hundreds of years, but do you see that cat trying to eat me </div>
</div> 

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (3).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">haha i hit thing with stick it die</div>
</div> 

On one side was an absolutely loaded, newly liberated country. I looked at my bunch of young children who just class-up’d, and all of a sudden, had a huge bankroll to buy weapons, and legendary items galore. Too bad they have to fight these animals that just attack you from across the map. On the other, you have a bunch of poor mercenaries, who are super well balanced, and fight well as a team; they could use iron weapons and be completely fine. It’s no wonder the Black Knight was and is my favorite character, because dang I needed help.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (4).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Yes eclipse is a huge animation, but do you see those camera angle shifts like, yes I’m watching him walk up and hit you with a nuke</div>
</div> 

Add in the detailed side stories of upcoming royalty, each fighting for their power. It’s a plot that I could fully understand at 12, and a plot that I can truly appreciate at nearly 30, odd deus ex machinas and mary sues be damned.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (5).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Remember kids, its not KYS if you get your ntr’d girlfriend to do it for you</div>
</div> 

Add onto that a super detailed and fun game, with memorable maps, weapons, and bosses, and an awesome endgame that allows you to experience the wide range of power levels in the world, it still stands as my favorite game of all time.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (6).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">When I played this game, I would literally re-enact the animations in real life. Yes, I put on the wrist strap, threw the wii-mote in the air, pretended to do a spin, and swung the wii-mote, all in time with the game.</div>
</div>

XCOM ENEMY WITHIN (2013)

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (7).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">XCOM Enemy Within for PC</div>
</div>

The progenitor of the modern squad-based tactics game. The mechanics of this game truly just ticked the right parts of my brain, and it’s something I still hunger for.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (8).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">it’s called aiming through the power of friendship</div>
</div> 

Let me just get something straight, though. The 99% accuracy joke does not apply to me. I play this game as a save-scumming loser, and I enjoy it. To me, that experience of making a super soldier and seeing how they run wild is the fun of these games. I’ve played it hardcore, and while it is its own style of fun, to me, I can balance and say what feels fair and fun, and I don’t need some developer on high to tell me some schlock about “emergent gameplay” to have fun.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (9).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">This always hits, I don’t care what you say.</div>
</div> 

The base building is, on paper, super simple, but it offers a fun, super concrete way to see your base’s advancement and how your team is growing. The UFO dogfight mini-game as well, while super simple, is exciting, especially when you see a completely new ship, experiencing a whole new wonder of seeing the invasion through tiny windows, real-time. In total, these small base-building experiences allow a narrow enough game experience where your brain can do the rest of the imagining of the actual experience.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (10).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Now if only you had animal ears on your soldiers, and the game would be perfect</div>
</div> 

Speaking of a full experience, that's where the writing closes it all out. Why did we give up greys from space? They are hilarious, and XCOM continues that camp perfectly. I still remember all of Vahlen’s questionable practices, and the weird aliens and blocky but beautiful armor and weapons made it all feel like a full art direction.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (11).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">This mod is required, just so you know</div>
</div> 

I’m personally just waiting for XCOM 3. I believe one day, a new one will come out of the ashes, and no number of overly complicated spiritual successors will dampen my excitement.

ELDER SCROLLS SKYRIM (2011)

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (12).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Skyrim for the PC</div>
</div>

My first game on PC. Well, I say PC, I mean a very underpowered Dell laptop that ran at 25 FPS, and I used a trackpad. But let's put that aside for now.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (13).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">How I imagine I played skyrim for the first time</div>
</div> 

I was, and still am, a completionist at heart. If a game has too many collectibles, or too many characters, someone can say “Just ignore it, and just mainline it. Just don’t use the overpowered things.” Yeah, sorry, that's just not an answer to me. Skyrim lies in that perfect happy medium, having tons of varied quests and areas that were just different enough to make me interested, and offering just varied enough rewards where each quest didn’t feel like a waste. Even a clear downgrade of a weapon is a good quest reward if it looks cool enough to put in my house.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (14).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Riften boys how we doin?</div>
</div> 

I’m at 550 hours, and while the game is buggy with quests, optimized weirdly, and super simplistic, it offered a world that was easy to grasp and a varied but grounded world map. This game was where I learned that I’m a map guy, and I was a master of geoguesser-Skyrim.

<div class="gallery-slide" style="max-width: 500px; margin: 20px auto;">
    <img src="https://lowcarboxyl.github.io/Will-Play/images/20260622 (15).jpg" alt="Description of your photo">
    <div class="slide-caption">Yeah I know this rock, water hungry trees, clear swamps of solitude easy 5000 points</div>
</div> 

Even now, I play games with a similar insane enjoyment of looking at every crease of a map. Skyrim just happened to hit a perfect time and place, at a tech level where the game was just open world enough.
